First Aid & CPR Instructions

Step-by-step emergency procedures that could save lives

How to Perform CPR (Adults)

1

Check for Responsiveness

Tap the person's shoulder and shout "Are you okay?" Look for normal breathing.

2

Call for Help

If unresponsive, call emergency services (112 in India) or ask someone to call.

3

Begin Chest Compressions

Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest. Place your other hand on top and interlock fingers. Push hard and fast (2 inches deep at 100-120 compressions per minute).

4

Give Rescue Breaths

After 30 compressions, tilt head back, lift chin, pinch nose shut, and give 2 breaths (1 second each).

5

Continue Until Help Arrives

Repeat cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until emergency services take over.

Note: For infants and children, use different techniques (see videos below).

First Aid for Choking

1

Recognize Choking

Look for inability to talk, cough, or breathe. The person may clutch their throat.

2

Encourage Coughing

If the person can cough, encourage them to keep coughing to try to dislodge the object.

3

Perform Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver)

Stand behind the person. Place a fist above their navel. Grasp your fist with your other hand and give quick upward thrusts.

4

Continue Until Object is Expelled

Repeat thrusts until the object comes out or the person becomes unconscious.

Treating Severe Bleeding

1

Apply Direct Pressure

Use a clean cloth or bandage to press directly on the wound.

2

Elevate the Wound

If possible, raise the injured area above heart level to reduce blood flow.

3

Add More Layers if Needed

If blood soaks through, add more cloth without removing the original dressing.

4

Seek Medical Help

For severe bleeding, call emergency services immediately after applying pressure.
